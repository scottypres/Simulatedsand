SimulatedSand

A physics-based falling sand simulator with 40+ elements, temperature physics, chemical reactions, and explosions.

Controls

Touch / Mouse

  • Tap & drag — Draw selected element
  • Hold in place — Continuously pour element
  • Pinch — Zoom in/out
  • Two-finger drag — Pan the view

Keyboard Shortcuts

SpacePause / Resume
SToggle Source mode
GFlip gravity
CClear canvas
Ctrl+ZUndo

Toolbar

  • ⏸ / ▶ — Pause or resume the simulation
  • ✕ — Clear the entire canvas
  • ↩ — Undo (up to 5 snapshots)
  • Brush size — Tap the number to show a slider (1–20)
  • S — Source mode (see below)
  • G — Flip gravity direction

Source Mode

Tap S to enter Source mode (canvas glows red, banner appears). While active:

  • Drawing places permanent spawners that endlessly produce the selected element
  • Sources appear as bright pulsing markers on the grid
  • Solids/liquids spawn downward, gases spawn upward
  • Select Erase and paint over sources to remove them
  • Works with every element — create sand faucets, lava falls, rain, gas vents, etc.

Tap S again to return to normal drawing mode.

Elements

Basic

Sand — Falls and piles. Melts into Glass at extreme heat. Lightning turns it to Glass.
Stone — Immovable solid. Melts into Lava at 1200°.
Fire — Burns flammable materials, produces Smoke, extinguished by Water.
Ice — Solid. Melts into Water above 5°.
Snow — Powder that melts into Water.
Salt — Powder. Dissolves in Water to create Saltwater.
Ash — Lightweight powder left behind by fire.
Ember — Hot falling particle that ignites flammable neighbors.
Dust — Light flammable powder. Can cause dust explosions.

Liquids

Water — Flows sideways. Freezes to Ice below 0°. Boils to Steam at 100°. Grows Plants.
Lava — Extremely hot liquid. Solidifies into Stone when cooled. Contact with Water creates Steam + Stone.
Oil — Flammable liquid. Lighter than Water (floats). Burns into Smoke.
Acid — Dissolves most materials. Resistance varies by element. Diamond is immune.
Saltwater — Slightly denser than Water. Freezes at -5°.

Gas

Smoke — Rises and dissipates over time.
Steam — Hot gas. Condenses back to Water when cooled.
Methane — Flammable gas. Explodes when ignited.

Explosives

Gunpowder — Flammable powder with small blast radius.
TNT — Powerful explosive. Chain-reacts with nearby explosives.
Napalm — Sticky flammable liquid. Burns intensely.
Nitro — Most powerful explosive. Massive blast radius.
Fuse — Burns slowly along a line. Use to create detonation chains.

Nature

Wood — Flammable solid. Burns to Charcoal.
Plant — Grows when touching Water. Flammable. Burns to Ash.
Seed — Falls like powder. Grows into Plant when it lands on a surface.
Mud — Created when Water mixes with Sand.
Charcoal — Flammable powder. Burns to Ash.

Materials

Metal — Strong conductor. Slowly rusts with Water. Melts at 1500°.
Rust — Weak powder created when Metal contacts Water.
Clay — Powder. Melts into Lava at extreme heat.
Glass — Transparent solid. Created by melting Sand or lightning strike.
Diamond — Indestructible. Immune to Acid. Highest hardness.
Wax — Melts into Oil at 60°. Flammable.
Cement — Powder that hardens into Concrete when touching Water.
Concrete — Strong immovable solid. Created from Cement + Water.

Special

Virus — Infects and converts adjacent particles. Eventually dies.
Clone — Copies the first element it touches, then endlessly produces it.
Void — Destroys all adjacent particles.
Lightning — Electric bolt. Superheats area, turns Sand to Glass, ignites explosives.
Plasma — Superheated gas. Heats everything nearby.

Tools

Erase — Remove particles (and sources in Source mode).
Wall — Indestructible barrier. Immune to everything.

Temperature

Every particle has a temperature. Heat conducts between neighbors based on each element's conductivity. Effects:

  • Hot particles glow orange-red
  • Cold particles glow blue
  • Crossing melt/boil/freeze thresholds triggers state changes
  • Fire, Lava, Ember, Lightning, and Plasma radiate extreme heat
  • Ice and Snow radiate cold
  • All particles slowly drift toward ambient temperature (22°)

Tips

  • Use Wall to build containers before pouring liquids
  • Create a Fuse trail leading to TNT for delayed detonation
  • Place Clone next to any element for infinite production
  • Use Source mode for rain (Water), sandstorms (Sand), volcanic eruptions (Lava)
  • Build an ecosystem: Seed + Water = growing plants
  • Oil floats on Water — set it on fire for a surface blaze
  • Flip Gravity to reverse everything
  • Void makes a great drain at the bottom of a scene
